The MPG Calculator

Simply predict a vehicle's MPG performance by its type of transmission, weight and horsepower.

The Final Model

The final GLM model was trained with caret and takes into consideration three variables:

  1. horsepower (hp),
  2. weight (wt) and
  3. the vehicle's transmission (am)
finalModel <- train(mpg ~ hp + wt + am,
                 data = mtcarsAdj,
                 method = "glm",
                 metric = "RMSE")


The Motor Trend Car Road Tests (mtcars) data was extracted from the 1974 Motor Trend US magazine, and comprises fuel consumption and 10 aspects of automobile design and performance for 32 automobiles (1973 and 1974 models). The data consists of 32 observations and 11 variables.

The Default MPG Performance

Once you open the MPG Calculator application, you will see the default MPG performance. You can then adjust the parameters and observe how the performance changes.

predictedMPG <- predict(finalModel, data.frame(hp = 120,
                                               wt = 3300 / 1000, 
                                               am = as.factor(1)))

paste(round(predictedMPG, 2), "Miles per Gallon")

## [1] "22.09 Miles per Gallon"
